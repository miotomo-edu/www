Meet Tomo

Tomo is from a planet called  Motara

Tomo is a Keeper of Knowledge, curious about Earth and entirely dependent on your child to make sense of it.

  • He arrives knowing nothing

    He does not know much about Earth yet, but he notices everything and asks wonderfully odd questions.

  • He only learns from your child

    If your child can't say it out loud, Tomo can't know it. So your child helps Tomo learn.

What it builds

Six skills, built through meaningful conversations

01

Critical thinking

Inference, cause and effect, weighing claims. Built turn by turn through questions that don't have one right answer.

02

Storytelling

Retell what happened, fill in the gaps, build a narrative. Tomo asks for the version only your child can tell.

03

Debating

Take a position. Defend it. Notice when someone changes your mind. We practice it like a sport, in low stakes.

04

Public speaking

Talking out loud, not into a chat box. Voice-first means fluency, turn-taking, and confidence at the level of the spoken word.

05

English proficiency

Vocabulary, sentence structure, and pronunciation, picked up the way fluent speakers really learn them: in the middle of a real conversation.

06

Diplomacy

Hear the other side, find common ground, and talk it out. When characters disagree, your child plays mediator and learns to bring them back together.

01 The role reversal

Your child is the expert.
Tomo is the student.

Most learning apps quiz children. Miotomo flips it.  Tomo doesn't know what a volcano is, or who Julius Caesar was, or why ice floats. He needs your child to tell him. This makes children take charge and they stop performing for a test and start thinking critically.

02 Topic-native characters

Conversations with the AI presenters inside the subject.

Every topic is hosted by characters who belong to it. Ancient Rome arrives as a senator and a soldier, both ready to argue. Dinosaurs show up as a T-Rex who remembers being one. Your child isn't told about the world, they're dropped into it.

03 The Circle

Every topic is a
five-episode journey.

A Circle is five 15-minute Dots. Each Dot is a story, a conversation, and a piece of a badge. Together they build to Teachtime, the moment your child sits down and teaches Tomo everything they've learned. It's a series, not a worksheet.

04 Made to be loved

Built by parents, for curious children 

  • Well designed topics

    Written, illustrated, and voice-acted to earn your child's attention.

  • Kids come back on their own

    They want to know what happens next, and because Miotomo gives them space to think, speak, and explore ideas.

  • A window in for parents

    A parent dashboard shows real progress, what your child argued, retold, and learned to say out loud.

05 Safety & privacy

Built to be trusted
with your child's voice.

  • A strict safety layer

    Every spoken turn passes a deterministic filter before the AI sees it. Unsafe content is blocked, not interpreted. Distress signals route to a trusted-adult message.

  • No human-mimicry

    Tomo is a friendly alien, not a person. We deliberately avoid characters that ask kids to form an emotional bond with something that isn't real.

  • Zero data retention

    We don't sell, share, or train models on your child's voice. Conversation transcripts exist only long enough to show you progress, then they're gone, encrypted in transit, deleted on a schedule, never used to improve anyone else's product.
